Abstract We show that an analysis of high-energy hadron-nuclear processes should take the nuclear effects in the nucleon channel into account to make correct conclusions about the contribution of multiquark systems, fluctuons, to the nuclear wave function and about the quark distributions in them. Analysis of these distributions indicates that the color degrees of freedom in a fluctuon are partially frozen out and with a high degree of probability a fluctuon contains one or two diquarks.
