Summary Isonicotinic acid hydrazide reacts with catechol in alkaline medium to give a pinkish-orange color which obeys Beer's law. This color reaction is specific for isonicotinic acid hydrazide and is used for its determination in blood or urine. The visual limit of identification is 1 μg/ml.
