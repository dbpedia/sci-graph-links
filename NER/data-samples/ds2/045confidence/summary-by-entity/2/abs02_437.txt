Abstract We use AdS/QCD holographic distributions amplitudes for the vector mesons $${\rho}$$ ρ and K * in order to predict the radiative decays $${B \to \rho \gamma}$$ B→ργ and $${B \to K^{*} \gamma}$$ B→K∗γ as well as the semileptonic decays $${B \to \rho l \nu}$$ B→ρlν and $${B \to K^{*} \mu^{ } \mu^{-}}$$ B→K∗μ μ- . For the radiative decays, we find that the AdS/QCD DAs offer the advantage of avoiding end-point singularities when computing power suppressed contributions. For the semileptonic decays $${B \to \rho l \nu}$$ B→ρlν and $${B \to K^{*} \mu^{ } \mu^{-}}$$ B→K∗μ μ- , we compare our predictions to the latest BaBar and LHCb data respectively.
