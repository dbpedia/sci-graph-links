Summary Horsechestnut anthers were isolated from flower buds in various stages of development and cultivated in the presence of auxin and kinetin. The anthers showed the capacity for androgenesis and evidence is presented that the plantlets developed from single microspores. Haploid karyotypes were observed in the root menstem cells in three plantlets.
