Abstract We present the new method of “vectorial regularisation” to prove kernel identities. This method is applied to derive both known kernel identities, e.g. $${\dot{\mathcal {B}}}_{xy}={\dot{\mathcal {B}}}_x{\widehat{\otimes }}_\varepsilon {\dot{\mathcal {B}}}_y$$ B˙xy=B˙x⊗^εB˙y , $${\mathcal {D}}'_{L^1,xy}={\mathcal {D}}'_{L^1,x}{\widehat{\otimes }}_\pi {\mathcal {D}}'_{L^1,y}$$ DL1,xy′=DL1,x′⊗^πDL1,y′ , as well as new ones: $${\dot{\mathcal {B}}}'_{xy}={\dot{\mathcal {B}}}'_x{\widehat{\otimes }}_\varepsilon {\dot{\mathcal {B}}}'_y$$ B˙xy′=B˙x′⊗^εB˙y′ and $${\mathcal {D}}_{L^1,xy}={\mathcal {D}}_{L^1,x}{\widehat{\otimes }}_\pi {\mathcal {D}}_{L^1,y}$$ DL1,xy=DL1,x⊗^πDL1,y .
