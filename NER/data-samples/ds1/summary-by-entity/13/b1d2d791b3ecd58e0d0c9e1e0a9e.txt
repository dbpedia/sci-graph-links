Abstract Advances in information and communications technology have created increased avenues to internationalize educational experiences for students on their home campuses. This article reports on a teaching strategy that used Blackboard to provide an ‘Internationalization at Home’ online exchange experience for Australian and American graduate school counseling students. Following an overview of internationalization at home and multicultural competencies in professional practice, a description of the teaching strategy is provided that includes consideration of its benefits and challenges.
