Abstract Let f be a holomorphic function in the disk U = {z: |z| < 1}, |f(z)| < 1 in U, let f(±1) = ±1 in the sense of angular limits, and let the angular Schwarzian derivatives S f (±1) exist. We establish an upper bound for the sum S f (−1) S f (1) under the assumption that the image f(U) does not contain open arcs of the pencil of circles arg[(1   w)/(1 − w)] = θ, −π/2 < θ < φ, with endpoints w = ±1 and $$\operatorname{Re} f''\left( 1 \right)   f'\left( 1 \right)\left( {1 - f'\left( 1 \right)} \right) = - \operatorname{Re} f''\left( { - 1} \right)   f'\left( { - 1} \right)\left( {1 - f'\left( { - 1} \right)} \right) = 0$$ This bound depends on φ and f′(±1) only.
