Abstract In this paper, we prove the following result: Let $${\mathcal{F}}$$ F be a family of meromorphic functions on a domain $${\Omega}$$ Ω such that every pair of members of $${\mathcal{F}}$$ F shares a set $${S:=\left\{\psi_1(z), \psi_2(z), \psi_3(z) \right\}}$$ S:=ψ1(z),ψ2(z),ψ3(z) in $${\Omega}$$ Ω , where $${\psi_j(z), \ j=1,2,3}$$ ψj(z),j=1,2,3 are meromorphic in $${\Omega.}$$ Ω. If for every $${f\in \mathcal{F}}$$ f∈F , $${f(z_0)\neq \psi_i (z_0)}$$ f(z0)≠ψi(z0) whenever $${\psi_i(z_0)=\psi_j(z_0)}$$ ψi(z0)=ψj(z0) for $${i,j\in \left\{1,2,3 \right\} (i\neq j)}$$ i,j∈1,2,3(i≠j) and $${z_0\in \Omega,}$$ z0∈Ω, then $${\mathcal{F}}$$ F is normal in $${\Omega}$$ Ω . This result generalizes a result of Fang and Hong (Bull Malays Math Sci Soc 23(2), 143–151, 2000) and in particular, it generalizes the most celebrated theorem of Montel i.e. Montel’s theorem.
