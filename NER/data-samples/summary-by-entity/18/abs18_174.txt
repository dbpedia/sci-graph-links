Abstract Suppose that X is a simple random walk on $${\mathbb {Z}}_n^d$$ Znd for $$d \ge 3$$ d≥3 and, for each t, we let $${\mathcal {U}}(t)$$ U(t) consist of those $$x \in {\mathbb {Z}}_n^d$$ x∈Znd which have not been visited by X by time t. Let $$t_{\mathrm {cov}}$$ tcov be the expected amount of time that it takes for X to visit every site of $${\mathbb {Z}}_n^d$$ Znd . We show that there exists $$0 < \alpha _0(d) \le \alpha _1(d) < 1$$ 0<α0(d)≤α1(d)<1 and a time $$t_* = t_{\mathrm {cov}}(1 o(1))$$ t∗=tcov(1 o(1)) as $$n \rightarrow \infty $$ n→∞ such that the following is true. For $$\alpha > \alpha _1(d)$$ α>α1(d) (resp. $$\alpha < \alpha _0(d)$$ α<α0(d) ), the total variation distance between the law of $${\mathcal {U}}(\alpha t_*)$$ U(αt∗) and the law of i.i.d. Bernoulli random variables indexed by $${\mathbb {Z}}_n^d$$ Znd with success probability $$n^{-\alpha d}$$ n-αd tends to 0 (resp. 1) as $$n \rightarrow \infty $$ n→∞ . Let $$\tau _\alpha $$ τα be the first time t that $$|{\mathcal {U}}(t)| = n^{d-\alpha d}$$ |U(t)|=nd-αd . We also show that the total variation distance between the law of $${\mathcal {U}}(\tau _\alpha )$$ U(τα) and the law of a uniformly chosen set from $${\mathbb {Z}}_n^d$$ Znd with size $$n^{d-\alpha d}$$ nd-αd tends to 0 (resp. 1) for $$\alpha > \alpha _1(d)$$ α>α1(d) (resp. $$\alpha < \alpha _0(d)$$ α<α0(d) ) as $$n \rightarrow \infty $$ n→∞ .
