Abstract This paper is devoted to the following chemotaxis system $$\left\{ \begin{array}{llll}u_t=\nabla\cdot(D(u)\nabla u)-\nabla\cdot(S(u)\nabla v),\quad &x\in \Omega,\quad t>0,\\ v_t=\Delta v-uv,\quad &x\in\Omega,\quad t>0,\end{array} \right.$$ ut=∇·(D(u)∇u)-∇·(S(u)∇v),x∈Ω,t>0,vt=Δv-uv,x∈Ω,t>0, under homogeneous Neumann boundary conditions in a smooth bounded domain $${\Omega\subset \mathbb{R}^n}$$ Ω⊂Rn ($${n\geq2}$$ n≥2 ), not necessarily being convex. There are some constants $${c_D > 0}$$ cD>0 , $${c_S > 0}$$ cS>0 , $${m\in\mathbb{R}}$$ m∈R and $${q\in\mathbb{R}}$$ q∈R such that $$D(u) \geq c_D(u+1)^{m-1} \quad\text{and} \quad S(u)\leq c_S(u+1)^{q-1}\quad for all \,\,\,u\geq0.$$ D(u)≥cD(u+1)m-1andS(u)≤cS(u+1)q-1forallu≥0. If $${q < m+\frac{n+2}{2n}}$$ q<m+n+22n , it is shown that the model possesses a unique global classical solution which is uniformly bounded; if $${q < \frac{m}{2}+\frac{n+2}{2n}}$$ q<m2+n+22n , the global existence of solution is established.
