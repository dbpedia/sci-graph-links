Abstract We consider the degenerate parabolic equation $${ \partial_t u = \triangle_{\lambda}u + f(u)}$$ ∂tu=▵λu+f(u) with Dirichlet boundary condition defined on a bounded domain $${\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^N}$$ Ω⊂RN , where $${\triangle_{\lambda}}$$ ▵λ , the so-called $${\triangle_{\lambda}}$$ ▵λ -Laplacian, is a subelliptic operator of the type $$\triangle_{\lambda} :=\sum_{i=1}^N\partial_{x_i}(\lambda^2_i\partial_{x_i}),\quad \lambda= (\lambda_1(x),\ldots,\lambda_N(x)).$$ ▵λ:=∑i=1N∂xi(λi2∂xi),λ=(λ1(x),…,λN(x)). In this paper, we will establish the global existence of solutions and its corresponding attractor with critical nonlinearity $$|f(u)-f(v)| \leq c|u-v|\left(1+|u|^{\frac{4}{Q-2}}+|v|^{\frac{4}{Q-2}} \right),\quad\forall~~u,v\in \mathbb{R}.$$ |f(u)-f(v)|≤c|u-v|1+|u|4Q-2+|v|4Q-2,∀u,v∈R.
