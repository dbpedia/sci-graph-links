Abstract This study identifies strategies used by non-English speaking medical students to comprehend scientific biomedical publications. It also describes the relationship among self-reported proficiency (both in English and in topic matter), perceived difficulty of the publication, reading time and subjective perceptions of learning and satisfaction.
