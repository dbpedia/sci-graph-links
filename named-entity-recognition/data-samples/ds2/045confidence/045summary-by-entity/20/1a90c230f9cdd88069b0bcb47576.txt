Summary The Hotz group H(G) and the Hotz monoid M(G) of an arbitrary grammar G=(V, X, P, S) are defined by H(G)=F(V∪X)/P and M(G) =(V∪X)*/P respectively. A language L⊂X* is called a language with Hotz isomorphism if there exists a grammar G with L=L(G) such that the natural homomorphism F(X)/L→H(G) is an isomorphism. The main result of this paper states that homomorphic images of sentential form languages are languages with Hotz isomorphism. This is a generalization of a result of Frougny, Sakarovitch, and Valkema on context-free languages. Hotz groups are used to obtain lower bounds for the number of productions which are needed to generate a language. Further it is shown that there are languages with Hotz isomorphism without being a homomorphic image of a sentential form language, and there are context-sensitive languages without Hotz isomorphism. The theory of Hotz monoids is used to get some results on languages generated by grammars with a symmetric set of rules.
