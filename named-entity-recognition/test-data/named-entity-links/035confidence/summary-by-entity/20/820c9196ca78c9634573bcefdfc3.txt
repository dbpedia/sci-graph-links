Abstract Since language has traditionally been treated as words and not actual behavior, it is proposed that the psychology of language should proceed in a better direction than heretofore. Instead of dealing with structures of letters or sounds, linguistic events should be studied as definite adjustments of organisms to two simultaneously operating stimulus objects.
